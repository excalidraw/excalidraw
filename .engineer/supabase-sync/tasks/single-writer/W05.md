## Implement
With `@supabase/supabase-js` mocked (reuse the existing mock pattern) and the flag stubbed on, assert the
role/handoff behavior at the seams (we can't run real Postgres, so drive via the mocked repo/RPC layer):
- **Two-session roles:** first session (lock free → claim acquired) → role writer, `viewModeEnabled===false`.
  Second session (claim not acquired) → role reader, `viewModeEnabled===true`, no pushBoard calls on edits.
- **Reader gate:** a reader's notifyChange/flush/syncNow do not call pushBoard / saveFiles.
- **Flush-success on handoff (M1):** writer with a pending takeover where flush SUCCEEDS → releaseLock called +
  demoted to reader; where flush FAILS → releaseLock NOT called, stays writer (no lost-edit window).
- **Takeover happy path:** requester calls takeOver → requestTakeover written; simulate readLockState showing
  lock freed → requester claims → role writer + applyRemoteScene (pull of flushed scene) invoked.
- **No client-clock liveness:** readLockState's `lock_live` drives role (mock returns lock_live true/false) —
  assert role follows `lock_live`, not any local time math.
Keep all assertions real; no skips.

PART A — HOOK level (`renderHook` + REAL timers), mirroring `useSupabaseSync.test.tsx`:
- **INVARIANT 1a** — a session that WINS the claim (`acquired:true`) → `role === "writer"`,
  `viewModeEnabled === false`, `lockLive`/`holderIsMe` true, and NO reader poll fires.
- **INVARIANT 1b** — a session that LOSES the claim (`acquired:false`) → `role === "reader"`,
  `viewModeEnabled === true`, `holderIsMe === false`, publishes the other holder.
- **INVARIANT 2 (reader gate)** — a reader's `notifyChange()` + `syncNow()` never reach the push
  pipeline: `pushBoard` and `saveFiles` are NOT called even after the full debounce window elapses;
  role/view-only unchanged.
- **INVARIANT 4 (takeover happy path)** — `takeOver()` writes `requestTakeover` + arms
  `takeoverInFlight`; then a poll showing the lock freed (`lockLive:false`, newer version) drives a
  claim (`acquired`) → `role === "writer"`, `viewModeEnabled === false`, `takeoverInFlight` cleared,
  and the flushed scene is pulled (`updateScene` invoked via `applyRemoteScene`).
- **INVARIANT 5a (server-clock liveness)** — `readLockState` `lockLive:true` (other writer) keeps a
  reader a reader across several poll cycles; with no takeover requested the poll never auto-claims
  (claim called exactly once, at start). Role follows the server verdict, not any client time math.
- **INVARIANT 5b (server-clock liveness)** — after a takeover request, `readLockState`
  `lockLive:false` (lease dead) lets the requester claim → writer. Promotion is driven purely by the
  server `lockLive` boolean.

PART B — ENGINE level (direct `SyncEngine` + FAKE timers), mirroring `syncEngine.test.ts`:
- **INVARIANT 3a (M1 flush-success on handoff)** — a writer with a pending takeover whose final flush

  final edit was pushed BEFORE the release.
- **INVARIANT 3b (M1 flush-failure on handoff)** — a writer whose final flush FAILS (network throw)
  → `releaseLock` NOT called, stays writer (no lost-edit window); the next heartbeat (connectivity
  restored) completes the handoff (release + demote). Both branches asserted real.
